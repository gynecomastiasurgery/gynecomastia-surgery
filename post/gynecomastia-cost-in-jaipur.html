Understanding the Procedure

Gynecomastia surgery involves the removal of excess breast tissue and fat to achieve a flatter and more defined chest. The procedure typically comprises two primary techniques:

  1. Liposuction: This technique targets fatty deposits in the chest area. A small incision is made through which a thin tube (cannula) is inserted to suction out excess fat. This technique is suitable for cases where the excess tissue is primarily composed of fat.
  2. Tissue Excision: For cases involving glandular tissue or skin excess, tissue excision is performed. This technique involves larger incisions, allowing the surgeon to directly remove the tissue or reposition the nipple to create a more natural appearance.
