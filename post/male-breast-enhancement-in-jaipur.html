Causes of Gynecomastia:

There are several factors that contribute to the development of gynecomastia. Hormonal imbalances, particularly an increase in estrogen levels relative to testosterone, can play a significant role. This can occur during adolescence, as well as later in life. Additionally, certain medications, medical conditions, and genetic predispositions can also contribute to the condition. Understanding the underlying causes is crucial in determining the appropriate course of action.

FAQs about Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

As you consider gynecomastia surgery in Jaipur, it’s natural to have questions about the procedure, outcomes, and recovery. Here are answers to some commonly asked questions to help you make an informed decision.

1. Is gynecomastia surgery painful?

Gynecomastia surgery discomfort is typically manageable with prescribed pain medication. While discomfort levels can vary from person to person, most patients report mild to moderate soreness and tightness in the chest area for a few days following the surgery. This discomfort gradually subsides as the healing process progresses.

2. Are the results of gynecomastia surgery permanent?

Yes, gynecomastia surgery provides long-lasting results. Once excess glandular tissue and fat are removed, the newly sculpted chest contour is designed to be permanent. However, it’s important to note that maintaining a healthy lifestyle through regular exercise and balanced nutrition can contribute to sustaining the results over time.

3. Will there be visible scars after the surgery?

While some scarring is inevitable, I employ advanced surgical techniques to minimize scarring and ensure that they fade over time. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas to reduce the visibility of scars. As the healing process progresses, scars typically become less noticeable, allowing you to confidently embrace your transformed chest appearance.

4. When can I resume physical activities after the surgery?

Patients can usually resume light activities, such as walking, within a week after gynecomastia surgery. However, it’s important to avoid strenuous exercises and activities that could strain the chest area for a few weeks. This precautionary measure is vital to support proper healing and achieve the best possible results.

5. Is gynecomastia surgery a safe procedure?

Gynecomastia surgery is generally safe when performed by a qualified and experienced plastic surgeon. I prioritize patient safety above all else and adhere to the highest medical standards. During the initial consultation, a thorough assessment of your medical history and current health status will be conducted to ensure that you are a suitable candidate for the procedure.
