Gynecomastia Aftercare: Navigating Recovery Recovery is a critical phase following gynecomastia surgery. While each patient’s experience may vary, here are some general guidelines to help you navigate this crucial period:

  • Immediate Post-Surgery: After the surgery, you’ll spend some time in the recovery area under observation. You may experience mild discomfort, swelling, and bruising, which are normal and will subside gradually.
  • Follow Medical Instructions: It’s important to adhere to the post-operative instructions provided by your surgeon. These may include wearing compression garments, taking prescribed medications, and attending follow-up appointments for monitoring.
  • Gradual Resumption of Activities: While rest is crucial for proper healing, light activities such as short walks are encouraged to improve blood circulation and prevent blood clots. However, avoid strenuous exercises and heavy lifting as advised by your surgeon.
