FAQs About Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

1. How long does the recovery process typically take after gynecomastia surgery?

The recovery timeline can vary depending on the extent of the procedure and individual healing rates. Generally, you can expect some discomfort and swelling in the first week, which gradually subsides. Most patients can return to light activities within a week and resume normal activities within a few weeks. I provide comprehensive post-operative instructions to guide you through a smooth recovery process.

2. What are the potential risks associated with male chest gland removal surgery?

Like any surgical procedure, gynecomastia surgery carries some risks. These can include infection, bleeding, scarring, and changes in sensation. However, with my expertise and attention to detail, these risks are minimized. During our consultation, I’ll discuss the potential risks and steps taken to ensure your safety and optimal results.

3. Will there be noticeable scars after the procedure?

While incisions are necessary for male chest gland removal surgery, I take great care to place them discreetly. Over time, the scars tend to fade and become less noticeable. Following proper post-operative care instructions and keeping the incision areas protected from sun exposure can aid in scar minimization.

4. Can gyno recur after surgery, and how can it be prevented?

In most cases, gynecomastia surgery provides lasting results. However, factors such as hormonal imbalances or weight gain can contribute to a recurrence. Maintaining a healthy lifestyle and weight can help prevent recurrence. Regular follow-up appointments and open communication with me can also address any concerns promptly.

5. When will I start to see the final results of the surgery?

Initial results will be visible once the swelling subsides, typically a few weeks after surgery. However, it may take a few months for the final results to fully manifest as your body heals and settles into its new contours. Patience is key, and I’m here to guide you through every stage of the recovery process.
