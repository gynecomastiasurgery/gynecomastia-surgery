The Hormonal Connection

Hormones play a pivotal role in the development of gynecomastia. An imbalance between estrogen (often considered a “female” hormone) and testosterone (a “male” hormone) can lead to the growth of breast tissue in males. This imbalance can occur during adolescence, leading to temporary breast enlargement, or later in life due to various medical conditions or medication use. It’s important to note that hormonal fluctuations are a natural part of life, and understanding them is key to addressing gynecomastia effectively.
