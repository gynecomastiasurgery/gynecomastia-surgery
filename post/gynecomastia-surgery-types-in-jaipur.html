1. What is the recovery period after gynecomastia surgery?

Recovery times vary based on the specific surgical approach and individual healing rates. Generally, patients can expect to resume light activities within about a week following the surgery. It’s advisable to avoid strenuous physical activities for a few weeks to ensure proper healing and optimal results.

2. Is gynecomastia surgery painful?

Discomfort after gynecomastia surgery is manageable and typically alleviated with prescribed pain medications during the initial recovery phase. Patients may experience mild soreness, swelling, and bruising, but these symptoms gradually subside as the body heals.

3. Are the results of gynecomastia surgery immediately visible?

While some improvement is noticeable immediately after surgery, patients should expect initial swelling. Over the course of a few weeks, as the swelling subsides, the full results become more apparent. Patience is key to experiencing the final outcome, which is typically achieved within a few months.

4. Can weight loss eliminate gynecomastia?

Weight loss may reduce the appearance of gynecomastia to some extent, particularly if excess fat contributes to the condition. However, for individuals with glandular tissue enlargement, weight loss might not fully address the issue. Surgical intervention is often necessary to achieve comprehensive and lasting results.
