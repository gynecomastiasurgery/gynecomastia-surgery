What Causes Gynecomastia?

The origins of gynecomastia can be attributed to a variety of factors, both physiological and hormonal. During puberty, hormonal imbalances can lead to temporary gyno, which often resolves itself. However, certain situations can result in persistent gynecomastia that requires intervention. Here are a few primary contributors:

  • Hormonal Changes: Fluctuations in testosterone and estrogen levels can disturb the balance and contribute to gyno.
  • Medications: Some medications, including certain antipsychotics and antiandrogens, may trigger the development of excess breast tissue.
  • Underlying Health Conditions: Conditions such as hypogonadism, hyperthyroidism, and liver disease can influence gyno development.
  • Lifestyle Factors: Substance abuse, particularly the excessive consumption of alcohol and anabolic steroids, can contribute to gynecomastia.

Recovery times can vary based on individual factors and the extent of the procedure. Generally, you can expect to return to your daily activities within a week or two after the surgery. However, more strenuous activities and exercises might need to be avoided for a few weeks to ensure proper healing and optimal results.
