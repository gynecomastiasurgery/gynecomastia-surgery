Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

If you’re considering gynecomastia surgery in Jaipur, you likely have several questions about the procedure, recovery, and results. Here are answers to some of the most commonly asked questions to help you make an informed decision:

1. What is the Recovery Process Like After Gynecomastia Surgery?

The recovery process after gynecomastia surgery varies from person to person. Generally, you can expect some swelling, bruising, and discomfort for the first few weeks. I will provide you with detailed post-operative instructions to ensure a smooth recovery. You might need to wear a compression garment to support healing and minimize swelling. It’s important to follow these instructions and attend follow-up appointments to monitor your progress.

2. Will There be Scarring After Gynecomastia Surgery?

While some scarring is inevitable after any surgery, I employ advanced techniques to minimize scarring as much as possible. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas, and over time, scars tend to fade and become less noticeable. I will also provide guidance on scar management to help optimize the healing process.

3. Are the Results of Gynecomastia Surgery Permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are generally permanent. The excess breast tissue is removed during the procedure, creating a more masculine chest contour. However, it’s important to maintain a healthy lifestyle to ensure long-lasting results. Fluctuations in weight or hormonal changes can potentially impact the outcomes, so maintaining a stable weight and overall health is recommended.

4. Is Gynecomastia Surgery Painful?

During the procedure, anesthesia is administered to ensure your comfort, and you won’t feel any pain. However, it’s normal to experience some discomfort during the initial recovery period. Prescription pain medications will be provided to manage any discomfort. Most patients find that any discomfort subsides gradually over the first week or two.

5. Can Gynecomastia Recur After Surgery?

In most cases, gynecomastia does not recur after surgery. The excess breast tissue is removed, and the remaining tissue is contoured for a more masculine appearance. However, factors like weight gain or hormonal changes can potentially lead to a recurrence. To minimize this risk, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and balanced nutrition, is recommended.
