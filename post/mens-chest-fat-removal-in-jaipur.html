The Science Behind Gynecomastia

Gynecomastia occurs due to hormonal imbalances, specifically an increase in estrogen levels relative to testosterone. This imbalance can lead to the development of excess breast tissue and give the appearance of enlarged breasts. While the exact causes can vary from person to person, factors such as genetics, medication, obesity, and certain medical conditions can contribute to the development of gynecomastia. Despite its relatively common occurrence, the condition can evoke feelings of embarrassment and hinder one’s ability to enjoy daily activities.
