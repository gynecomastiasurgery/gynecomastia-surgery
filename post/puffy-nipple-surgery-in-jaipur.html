Q1: What is the ideal age for undergoing Gynecomastia Surgery?

A: Gynecomastia Surgery is typically recommended for individuals whose bodies have fully matured. This usually occurs around the age of 18 or older. It’s important to wait until your body has completed its growth cycle to ensure optimal results from the procedure.

Q2: Is the procedure performed under general anesthesia?

A: Yes, Gynecomastia Surgery is generally performed under general anesthesia. This ensures that you are comfortable and pain-free throughout the procedure. Anesthesia also allows the surgical team to work with precision, ensuring safety and optimal outcomes.

Q3: Will there be visible scars after the surgery?

A: While some scarring is inevitable with any surgical procedure, our skilled surgeons employ techniques to minimize visible scarring. Incisions are strategically placed to be as discreet as possible, and post-operative care instructions are provided to promote optimal healing and scar management.

Q4: What is the recovery period like after Gynecomastia Surgery?

A: Recovery times can vary depending on the extent of the procedure and the individual’s healing rate. Generally, most patients can resume light activities within a week. Strenuous exercises and activities that could strain the chest area are typically restricted for a few weeks to ensure proper healing.

Q5: Are the results of Gynecomastia Surgery permanent?

A: The results of Gynecomastia Surgery are generally long-lasting. However, it’s important to maintain a healthy lifestyle to ensure the longevity of the outcomes. This includes maintaining a stable weight, engaging in regular exercise, and following a balanced diet.
