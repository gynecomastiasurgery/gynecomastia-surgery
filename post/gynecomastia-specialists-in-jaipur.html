FAQ’s: Commonly Asked Questions about Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

Navigating the world of gynecomastia surgery can raise a multitude of questions. To provide clarity and insights, here are answers to some of the frequently asked questions surrounding gyno solutions in Jaipur:

What causes gynecomastia in men?

Gynecomastia can be triggered by a variety of factors, including hormonal imbalances, genetics, certain medications, and obesity. An overgrowth of glandular tissue or excess fat can lead to the appearance of enlarged male breasts. Understanding the root cause is crucial for devising an effective treatment plan.

Is gynecomastia surgery a safe procedure?

Yes, gynecomastia surgery is generally safe when performed by a skilled and experienced surgeon like Dr. Vishal Purohit. Pre-operative assessments, proper anesthesia, and adherence to post-operative care instructions contribute to minimizing risks. Dr. Purohit’s extensive expertise ensures a safe and successful surgical experience.

How long does recovery take after surgery?

Recovery timelines can vary depending on the extent of the procedure and individual healing rates. Typically, patients can expect to resume light activities within a week and gradually return to regular activities over a few weeks. Dr. Purohit provides detailed post-operative instructions to facilitate a smooth recovery process.

Are the results of gynecomastia surgery permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are generally permanent. Once excess glandular tissue and fat are removed, the chest contour is improved, and the likelihood of a recurrence is minimal. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle and stable weight is essential to preserve the results long term.

Can gynecomastia reoccur after surgery?

In most cases, gynecomastia surgery provides lasting results. However, factors such as significant weight gain, hormonal imbalances, or certain medications can contribute to a recurrence. Dr. Purohit addresses these concerns during the consultation, helping patients understand how to minimize the risk of recurrence.
