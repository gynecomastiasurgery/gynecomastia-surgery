FAQs about Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

As a seasoned plastic surgeon specializing in gynecomastia treatment, I understand that you may have questions about the procedure, recovery, and outcomes. Here are some commonly asked questions about gynecomastia surgery in Jaipur:

1. What causes gynecomastia?

Gynecomastia can be attributed to a variety of factors, including hormonal imbalances, genetics, certain medications, and underlying medical conditions. Hormonal changes during puberty, aging, or as a result of certain medical conditions can lead to the development of excess breast tissue in males.

2. Is non-surgical treatment effective?

Absolutely. Non-surgical approaches, such as liposuction for male breast reduction in Jaipur, have proven to be effective in reducing excess fat and restoring a more masculine chest appearance. These techniques are minimally invasive and can provide natural-looking results without the need for surgical incisions.

3. How long is the recovery period?

The recovery period after gynecomastia treatment can vary depending on the specific procedure performed. Non-surgical options like liposuction typically involve a shorter recovery time compared to surgical excision. In many cases, patients can resume light activities within a week after non-surgical treatments.

4. Will there be visible scarring?

Non-surgical techniques like liposuction involve small incisions that result in minimal scarring. These incisions are strategically placed and tend to heal well over time, often becoming barely noticeable. Surgical excision may involve slightly larger incisions, but I take care to place them in inconspicuous locations to minimize visibility.

5. Are the results permanent?

With proper post-treatment care and maintenance, the results of gynecomastia treatment can be long-lasting. Following my recommendations for lifestyle and exercise can help maintain the achieved results. However, it’s important to note that individual factors and changes in weight can influence the longevity of results.
