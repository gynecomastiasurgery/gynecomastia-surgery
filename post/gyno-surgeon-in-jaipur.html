  4. Anesthesia and Medical Staff: Anesthesia fees and the expertise of medical staff involved in the procedure are included in the overall cost.
  5. Post-Operative Care: Follow-up appointments, medications, and post-operative garments are considerations in the cost estimation.

What Causes Gynecomastia?

Gynecomastia can be attributed to a variety of factors, including hormonal imbalances, genetics, certain medications, and obesity. Hormonal changes during puberty, for instance, can lead to temporary gynecomastia in adolescents. Identifying the underlying cause is crucial to determine the most appropriate treatment approach.

Is Gynecomastia Surgery Painful?

During gynecomastia surgery, you will be under anesthesia, ensuring that you do not experience pain during the procedure. Mild discomfort or soreness is common during the initial days of recovery, but this can be managed with prescribed pain medication. Rest assured, our priority is to make your experience as comfortable as possible.

How Long Is the Recovery Period?

Recovery times can vary based on the extent of the procedure and individual factors. Most patients can resume light activities within a week, although strenuous exercise and heavy lifting should be avoided for a few weeks. It’s important to follow post-operative care instructions diligently to ensure a smooth and efficient recovery.

Are the Results Permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are generally permanent. Once the excess glandular tissue is removed and the chest contour is restored, you can expect a more masculine appearance. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and a balanced diet, is essential to sustain the results over time.

Are Scars Noticeable?

Scarring after gynecomastia surgery is minimal and strategically placed to be discreet. The incisions are made along natural contours and usually fade over time. While some initial redness and mild scarring may be present, the scars become less noticeable as they continue to heal.
