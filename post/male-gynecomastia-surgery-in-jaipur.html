FAQs About Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

As an experienced MCh Plastic Surgeon in Jaipur, I understand that you may have questions and concerns about gynecomastia surgery. Let’s address some of the most commonly asked questions to help you make informed decisions about your journey towards regaining confidence and embracing a more confident contour.

1. What is the recovery period after gynecomastia surgery?

The recovery period varies based on the extent of the procedure and individual healing factors. Generally, patients can expect to resume light activities within a week after surgery. Strenuous exercises and heavy lifting should be avoided for a few weeks to ensure optimal healing. I provide personalized recovery guidelines to each patient, ensuring a smooth and comfortable healing process.

2. Will there be visible scarring after the procedure?

While some scarring is inevitable, I take great care to place incisions in discreet areas to minimize their visibility. Over time, scars tend to fade and become less noticeable. Following proper scar care instructions, such as avoiding direct sunlight and applying recommended ointments, can further promote favorable scar healing.

3. Can gynecomastia recur after surgery?

In most cases, gynecomastia correction provides long-lasting results. However, it’s essential to maintain a healthy lifestyle and stable weight to prevent potential recurrence. I offer guidance on maintaining results during follow-up appointments and emphasize the importance of a balanced diet and regular exercise.

4. Are the results of surgery permanent?

Gynecomastia surgery can provide permanent results when performed by an experienced surgeon. The excess breast tissue and fat are removed during the procedure, leading to a more masculine chest contour. While significant weight fluctuations or hormonal imbalances can influence results, maintaining a healthy lifestyle can help ensure the longevity of your improved appearance.

5. When can I resume regular activities after the procedure?

Most patients can return to light activities and work within a week after surgery. However, it’s essential to avoid strenuous exercises and heavy lifting for a few weeks to allow proper healing. I provide individualized guidelines during the recovery period to ensure you can gradually resume your regular activities while minimizing any potential discomfort.
