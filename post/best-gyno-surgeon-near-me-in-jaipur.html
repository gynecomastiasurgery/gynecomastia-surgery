Q1: What is the ideal age for Gynecomastia Surgery?

Gynecomastia Surgery is typically recommended for individuals who have reached their late teens or early adulthood. This is because hormonal fluctuations that contribute to Gynecomastia often stabilize by this time. However, the ideal age can vary based on individual cases. During our consultation, I assess your specific situation to determine if you’re a suitable candidate for the procedure.

Q2: Will the surgery leave visible scars?

I employ surgical techniques that minimize scarring as much as possible. Incisions are strategically placed around the areola or in inconspicuous areas to ensure that scars are discreet. Over time, scars tend to fade and become less noticeable. Following post-operative care instructions also plays a crucial role in promoting optimal healing and reducing the visibility of scars.

Q3: How long is the recovery period after the surgery?

Recovery duration can vary based on factors such as the extent of the surgery, individual healing capacity, and adherence to post-operative care instructions. Generally, patients can expect to resume light activities within a week or two after surgery. Strenuous physical activities and heavy lifting should be avoided for about four to six weeks to ensure proper healing.

The surgery aims to provide long-lasting results. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and a balanced diet, is essential to prevent Gynecomastia from recurring.
